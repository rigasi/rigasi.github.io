Unit 2


The focus of unit 2 was on defining a research proposal. Besides defining the research question itself this unit raised interesting topics such as the usefulness and contribution that each research proposal ought to make. This might  seem to be an obvious point but I am sure that many choose their proposals considering what might be easier rather than usefull. 


Activity

Start planning your literature review based on your chosen topic from the list provided in Unit 1. Your outline of the literature review can be submitted for formative feedback in Unit 4 before final submission in Unit 7. You can use the guidance document for the outline submission and to prepare your review.

You should also take some time to consider your research proposal which could be based around the area you have chosen to investigate in your literature review or the topic of your capstone project (MSc students). Your outline of the proposal can be submitted for formative feedback in Unit 8 before final submission in Unit 10. Units 8 and 9 will be focussed on analysing data which, along with research methods, will also need to be considered in your presentation.

Choosing a topic was not easy and I had to spend some time and try to imagine how would I proceed based on a potential choice. In the end I chose a more generic topic under the following title: “The use of cloud computing for building effective information systems in the aeronautical industry”. My choice was based on the fact that I know the industry very well and also I had a strong incentive to carry out some literature review research in this area. One of the issues I encountered was that there was a limited number of publications in the field and two of them were of poor quality. I was surprised to find out that work with so many inconsistencies could have been published. Poor quality ranged from methodological errors to misleading information.

I had carried out a literature review in another field in the past and therefore I could progress on a higher pace, nevertheless it is always a challenge reviewing literature and understanding what might be relevant for the research and where the literature gaps are.
