Transmitting fake signals to aircraft to cause an activation of the traffic collision and avoidance system which would result in the aircraft maneuvering to avoid the fake target. Extrapolating this idea another possibility would be to involve fake targets between actual traffic and though a series of inputs cause the fake targets to move and create a scenario in which actual aircraft perform a series of evasive maneuvers which lead them to an actual collision. The data collection would be based on gathering data from actual simulations using the Eurocontrol CAVEAT software which emulates the behaviour of aircraft in collision situations using TCAS.
For investigating the first hypothesis , data can be gathered regarding the activation flags of TCAS in the presence of validated opposite threats which correspond to fake targets. This would show that a series of fake targets indeed activate TCAS within certain parameters of time proximity leading to a second state in which TCAS responds with a resolution advisory.
For investigating the second hypothesis, data can be gathered with respect to scenarios in which fake targets are projected between actual traffic to try to lead the actual traffic to a collision. In such a simulation scenario data on the CPA (closest point of approach) can be collected an analysed. A CPA corresponding up to twice the size of the aircraft from a central point would correspond to a collision whereas higher CPAs would correspond to a near miss.
Collecting data from the two scenarios would hopefully show whether the two hypothesis are accepted or rejected.
