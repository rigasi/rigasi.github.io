Unit 11


Unit 11 focused on developing an e- Portfolio. As I have worked systematically throughout the course , collecting the necessary bits and pieces of information was not difficult. I revisited each unit and my notes to reflect on what I have learned in each unit, areas with which I struggled and areas in which I have developed. Here are some key notes from my Journey:

Literature review:

This has been a very good exercise in synthesising information and consolidating arguments. Arguably literature review is a time consuming task which requires good organisational skills and the ability to organise knowledge in different domains so as to be in a position to synthesise arguments. The tasks of identifying gaps in existing knowledge and being critical of the work of others is always demanding and sometimes it inevitable stretches the boundaries of our own understanding of knowledge.

Research Proposal:

This was yet another demanding exercise as it had to be realistic and it required partly some serious research. I chose a very complex but interesting topic in the field of cyber security in physical systems, namely, in air traffic control. Due to the complexity of the topic I spent an excessive amount of time in the technical details of it and in literature review and thus I did not have sufficient time for the actual presentation. I could develop my arguments but I should have invested more time in polishing it and also on the content itself , to be more critical of my methodology , existing literature review etc.

E-portfolio:

The format of my e-portfolio is more like a diary with reflective touches than anything else. This format has served me well in the past and I know that it might not be the preferred format but it works for me as it enables me to look back into the challenges I encountered in each unit and reflect on those.
