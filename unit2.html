The focus of unit 2 was diving into the risk management process and looking into the different types of assessments , qualitative vs quantitative.

The unit 2 seminar focused on a discussion around Spears and Barki (2010) on the two assessment types. Below are my notes from unit 2 activity:

Unit 2 Seminar Activity

A.How did the authors use both Qualitative and Quantitative assessment approaches? What benefits did each approach yield?

The authors used a large pool of users in order to approach everybody involved in the process. Involving such a diverse group of people provided a lot of data but also preserved an element of subjectivity in the approach.

B.What do the authors list as the advantages of involving users in the risk management process?
You get multiple feedback from different sides and users feel that they are involved in the process, they feel part of the process as disvussed in the Buy-in theory of user participation (Markus and Mao 2004). User participation also raises the organizational awareness of security risks and controls within targeted business processes and facilitated greater alignment of SRM with business objectives, values, and needs.

C. Based on the findings of the research,

C1. How will the lack of user access affect the risk assessment you will carry out as part of your assessment?
A lack of user access will affect the outcome of the assessment as users will not have enough feedback to relate to the business processes and risk awareness. Without having access and without a understanding of the processes/areas users might not be able to form a complete picture and therefore participate in the risk assessment effectively.

C2. Will it affect the choice of Qualitative vs. Quantitative assessment methods you utilise?
Without sufficient user input it is not possible to conduct a qualitative assessment which is more subjective and opinion based than a quantitative assessment.

C3. How might you mitigate any issues encountered?
Especially with respect to qualitative assessments tacit knowledge and pre-conception/biases are important as they can have an impact on the risks identified as well as their classification. As an element of subjectivity will always remain it is important to consider especially any biases which can lead to locking in/focusing on specific ideas and risks and therefore missing the big picture.
